Retail Performance Analysis

Uncovering operational patterns and revenue opportunities in retail sales data

📊 Tools: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn
⏱️ Scope: 4-year historical analysis
📈 Dataset: 9,800 orders

Business Problem

The company wants to increase revenue but lacks clear visibility into:
- Which regions drive the most value
- When demand peaks
- Which products contribute most to growth

Core Question: How can the company increase revenue using historical sales data?

Rather than simply describing the data, this analysis focuses on translating patterns into recommendations — the kind of insights that inform quarterly planning, logistics optimization, and targeted marketing campaigns.

Dataset Overview

9,800 Total Orders (2015-2018)
16 Features Analyzed
0.01% Data Quality Issues

The dataset covers customer orders across multiple product categories, sub-categories, regions, and customer segments over a 4-year period. Data quality was exceptionally high — no missing values, minimal duplicates (1 record), and logically consistent timestamps, allowing the analysis to focus on pattern discovery rather than extensive cleaning.

Key Insights

Seasonal Demand Concentration

Order volume peaks toward year-end, with September, November, and December consistently dominating sales. This seasonal surge likely reflects back-to-school (September) and holiday shopping (November-December) behaviors.

Actionable Impact: Inventory and staffing should ramp up in Q3-Q4 to capture 40%+ of annual demand.

Weekday Ordering Patterns Evolve Over Time

2015-2016: Tuesday, Saturday, and Sunday dominated order volume, with Monday/Thursday/Friday consistently underperforming.

2017-2018: Monday and Friday emerged as top-performing days, while Wednesday dropped off. This shift suggests changing customer habits, potentially driven by promotional campaigns, delivery schedule preferences, or weekend browsing habits.

Actionable Impact: Marketing campaigns should adapt to these patterns, target Monday/Friday promotions in recent years, and investigate why Wednesday lost momentum.

Regional Sales Disparities

The West region consistently outperforms others in order volume and revenue contribution, having the east as a runner up. This disparity may be due to demographic factors, regional marketing efforts, or distribution center proximity.

Actionable Impact: Conduct a deeper dive into regional demographics and marketing strategies. Consider reallocating resources to boost underperforming regions or further capitalize on strong markets.

Product Category Performance

Technology consistently drives higher revenue per order compared to others (e.g., Office Supplies). This suggests a skewed sales distribution where a smaller number of high-value products contribute disproportionately to revenue.

Actionable Impact: Focus upselling and cross-selling efforts on high-value categories. Consider bundling lower-performing categories with popular items to boost overall sales.

Sales Distribution Heavily Right-Skewed

Most orders cluster in the lower price range, with occasional high-value outliers. This indicates that the business is driven by a high volume of low-value transactions, with a small number of high-value orders contributing disproportionately to total revenue.

Actionable Impact: Segment customers into "volume buyers" vs. "high-value buyers" for differentiated retention strategies. Upsell/cross-sell campaigns should target the high-value segment.

Strategic Takeaways

  • Inventory Planning: Concentrate stock replenishment in Q3-Q4 to align with 40%+ annual demand surge
  • Promotional Timing: Shift weekday campaigns toward Monday/Friday based on recent ordering trends
  • Customer Segmentation: Differentiate marketing strategies for volume buyers (retention) vs. high-value customers (upsell)

Analytical Approach

Data Preparation

  • Validated data integrity: 0 missing values, 1 duplicate removed, logical timestamp consistency confirmed
  • Engineered delivery time feature from order-to-ship dates
  • Verified categorical variables for typos and business logic

Analysis Techniques

  • Temporal Analysis: Year-over-year comparisons, monthly/weekday aggregations
  • Distribution Analysis: Outlier detection, skewness assessment for sales values
  • Categorical Profiling: Segment performance across regions, product categories, customer types
  • Operational Metrics: Delivery time calculations and validation
